Italian Sunday Sauce

Start this sauce on Sunday morning and fill your kitchen with amazing aromas that will have your family asking when dinner's
going to be ready! It's full of rich, sweet tomato flavor, subtle herbs, and savory,
tender meat that's cooked right in the sauce. Ladle it over your favorite pasta and serve with buttery garlic bread.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds pork neck bones
  • 2 teaspoons kosher salt, plus more to taste
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil, divided
  • 1 ¼ pounds Italian sausage links
  • 1 ½ cups finely chopped white onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 (12 ounce) cans tomato paste
  • 1 (28 ounce) can tomato puree
  • 1 (28 ounce) can crushed tomatoes
  • 7 cups water
  • 1 tablespoon white sugar, or more to taste
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 tablespoon dried basil
  • ½ teaspoon dried oregano
  • 12 large cooked meatballs

Steps

  1. Season the pork neck bones with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat 1 tablespoon olive oil in a large pot and brown the pork on all sides, then remove and set aside.
  3. Brown the Italian sausage links in the same pot, then remove and set aside.
  4. Add the remaining olive oil to the pot, then sauté the chopped onion and minced garlic until soft.
  5. Stir in the tomato paste and cook for 1.2 minutes.
  6. Add tomato puree, crushed tomatoes, and water, then stir in sugar, bay leaf, basil, and oregano.
  7. Return the pork and sausage to the pot and bring to a simmer.
  8. Cover and cook on low heat for 1.5.2 hours until the pork is tender.
  9. Add the cooked meatballs to the sauce and heat through.
  10. Serve hot with your choice of pasta or bread.
